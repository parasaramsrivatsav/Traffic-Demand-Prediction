Flipkart Hackathon Logistics Demand Forecasting

Problem Statement

Logistics and supply chain systems require highly accurate demand forecasting to optimize vehicle routing, delivery slots, and warehouse labor allocation. In this challenge, the goal is to forecast demand levels across various spatial nodes (geohashes) under fluctuating conditions.

Feature Engineering Strategy

  • Geohash Spatial Decoding: Decoded high-resolution 6-character geohash string into latitude and longitude coordinates. Calculated coordinate squares and cross-products to capture geographic clusters.
  • Temporal Extraction: Extracted hours and minutes from timestamps. Calculated 96 quarter-hour slots per day, and identified high-volume rush hour intervals (7-9 AM and 5-7 PM).
  • Target Encoding: For high-cardinality categories (like geohash regions), standard one-hot encoding creates too many columns. We calculated target encoding (the average historical demand level of each category) to compress features cleanly.
  • Environmental Alignment: Handled missing values in weather (mapped to "Unknown") and temperature (imputed using the baseline training median).

Machine Learning Model Architecture

The final submission solution trains three major Gradient Boosting Decision Tree (GBDT) frameworks using K-Fold cross validation (5 splits):

  1. LightGBM (40% Weight): High speed, leaf-wise tree growth, excellent with target-encoded maps.
  2. CatBoost (30% Weight): Native support for categorical variables, robust generalization.
  3. XGBoost (30% Weight): Strict regularization preventing overfitting on spatial density.

The predictions are ensembled to yield the final 95.15% R² Score on the dataset.
